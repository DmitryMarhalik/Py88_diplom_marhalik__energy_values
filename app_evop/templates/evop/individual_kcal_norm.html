The calculation will be made according to the Mifflin-San Geor Formula:

    The Mifflin-San Geor formula, developed by a group of American nutritionists under Under the guidance of Doctors Mifflin and San Geor, it gives out the required number of calories (kcal) per day for each individual.It was withdrawn in 2005

Activity:

minimal – activity, sedentary work that does not require significant physical exertion

weak – low activity level: intense exercise for at least 20 minutes once or three times a week. This can be cycling, jogging, basketball, swimming, ice skating, etc. If you do not exercise regularly, but maintain a busy lifestyle that requires frequent walking for a long time, then choose this coefficient

middle – moderate activity level: intensive training for at least 30-60 minutes three to four times a week (any of the sports listed above);

heavy – heavy or time-consuming activity: intensive exercises and sports 5-7 days a week. Labor-intensive occupations are also suitable for this level, they include construction work (bricklaying, carpentry, etc.), employment in agriculture, etc.;

extreme – extreme level: includes extremely active and/or very energy-intensive activities: sports with an almost daily schedule and several workouts during the day; very time-consuming work, for example, raking coal or a long working day on the assembly line. This level of activity is often very difficult to achieve.

*It is worth noting that this formula is relevant only for people aged 13 to 80 years.
